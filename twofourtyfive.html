Which Museums Look Good

So you don't have time to actually go to the museums and like learn history or appreciate art or whatever people do at museums, but you still want people to know you went to a museum. Fear not, we've got the definitive numbers for museum-Insta bang for buck. Here's 15 of the most hashtagged museums:

It's cool to know where most people are going for their #coolmuseumsnaps, but which museums give the best results? I scraped over 10000 posts (see methodology section for source data if you're curious) and compared the number of likes on a user's museum posts to the average number of likes on all their other posts, here's the ordering of how museum Insta posts stack up:

With an average of -1.950 likes on average compared to other posts on a user's accounts, it turns out people might not like endless museum snaps cluttering their feed! Moreover, a standard deviation of 6.792 implies even more heavily that there's no real link between likes and going to museums at all. We can also pretty clearly see that there's no correlation between the number of posts at a location and the number of likes it gets relative to its peers. Does this look any better when we segment by museum type?

There's a little more clustering here, but really the only thing we learn is something we already knew: art posts can be hit or miss and history posts tend to be scroll-past-able. There's hope for the more niche categories, like science or specialty museums, so be sure to look head to some of the smaller and different shaped marks on the Tripadvisor map!
